Experiential Course on Entreprenuership

York University X Technion University, Summer 2018

One of best experiences of my life...

In my first year, in the middle of Canada's longest post-secondary strike, I participated in flagship, experiential entreprenuerial course, in Israel. It was one of most invaluable adventure/learning experience I have been to, and heres how it went.

Israel: The startup nation

Israel's culture and history has made it a global hub of innovation and entrepreneurial spirit. Studying about the startup model here was like drinking from nectar of inventiveness.

A crucial part of this trip was understanding what made this possible. Which is why, before our academic studies, cultural and historical lessons took place. Starting from Tel Aviv, the face of modern Israel, we saw the clear potential and tangible potrait of growth that comes with a resilient, industrious and adapative approach.

In contrast, by seeing the old historical sites like Old City (Jerusalem) and Masadah, and museums gave us insight into the region's multicultural, vibrant and rich human history. It was evident that the Middle-East, at the cross-roads of ideas, is melting pot of ideas.

Haifa, second largest city was almost polar opposite of Tel Aviv. It was more laidback, classical and ascetic in nature. It was a perfect place for our studies, allowing for an immersive learning and contemplative cultivation of ideas.

What did I learn?

We spent about a week of lectures, visiting startups and speaker sessions at the Technion University in Haifa. I learned the following during this time.

Idea Generation Machine

Market & User Studies

Business, Pricing and Revenue Models

Entrance, Expansion and Exit Strategies

Unique Value Proposition
